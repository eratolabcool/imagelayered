# SEO Growth Requirements

These requirements are mandatory for every ProductViews website project, page, feature, and content update. The goal is simple: every page should have a clear ranking target, enough page-level competitiveness, and a role in the site's growth system.

## Core Principles

1. Every link can pass authority, whether it is an internal link or an external backlink.
2. Google's smallest ranking unit is the page. The homepage is also just a special page.
3. A page can rank only when its competitiveness is strong enough for the keyword it targets.
4. Every keyword search result is a leaderboard. Our page is competing directly with the pages already ranking there.

## Page-Level SEO Rule

Every important page must target one primary keyword cluster.

Do not combine unrelated big keywords on the same page. For example, do not make a homepage target both "AI image" and "AI video" if the page cannot satisfy both search intents deeply. A more focused page has a better chance to rank than a page that mentions every product capability.

## Required Page Planning

Before creating or rewriting a page, define:

- Primary keyword
- Secondary keyword variations
- Search intent
- Target audience
- Page type: homepage, money page, blog post, comparison page, template page, use-case page, or pricing page
- Main conversion action
- Pages that should internally link to it
- External link or backlink plan if the keyword is competitive

If these are unclear, the page should not be shipped as an SEO target.

## On-Page SEO Requirements

Every SEO-targeted page must:

- Put the primary keyword or a close variation in the title, H1, meta description, and opening section.
- Stay tightly focused on one search intent.
- Answer the user's real question better than a thin landing page.
- Include enough useful information, examples, workflows, FAQs, or comparisons to deserve ranking.
- Include internal links to relevant ProductViews pages.
- Include a clear CTA to the product, pricing, email list, or case-study submission.
- Avoid keyword stuffing and unrelated feature lists.

## Competitiveness Checklist

Before expecting a page to rank, check:

1. Is the on-page topic focused enough?
2. Is the content more useful than weak pages already ranking?
3. Does the page have internal links from stronger pages such as homepage, pricing, blog index, or relevant use-case pages?
4. Does the page satisfy user intent quickly above the fold?
5. Does the page load and render correctly on mobile and desktop?
6. Does the page have enough trust signals, examples, visuals, or proof?
7. If competitors are stronger, does the page have or need backlinks?

## Internal Linking Requirements

High-value pages must receive internal links from:

- Homepage, when strategically relevant
- Blog index
- Related blog posts
- Related use-case pages
- Pricing page, when tied to purchase intent
- Tool pages such as `/ecommerce`

Internal links should use descriptive anchor text. Avoid vague anchors like "click here" when a keyword-rich anchor is natural.

## External Link and Backlink Requirements

Internal links are the first step. If a page still cannot compete, build external links to that exact page, not only the homepage.

Use backlinks when:

- The keyword SERP is competitive.
- Existing ranking pages have stronger domains or more links.
- The page is a money page or high-value SEO article.
- The page has already been optimized on-page and internally linked.

Backlink growth should be steady. For most competitive keywords, expect three to four months of consistent link building before visible ranking movement.

## Keyword Focus Rules

- Homepage should target the core product category, not every possible feature.
- Money pages should target commercial-intent keywords.
- Blog posts should target one problem, comparison, checklist, template, or guide intent.
- Use-case pages should target one audience or platform, such as Shopify stores, Amazon sellers, or ecommerce catalog teams.
- If two keyword intents are meaningfully different, create two pages and link them together.

## SERP Review Requirement

For every serious keyword, review the search results before finalizing the page.

Check:

- What page types are ranking?
- Are they homepages, blog posts, listicles, templates, tools, or category pages?
- How much content depth do they have?
- What questions do they answer?
- What proof, visuals, or examples do they include?
- Which ranking pages look weak enough for us to beat?
- What would make our page meaningfully better?

The goal is not to publish a page. The goal is to replace a weaker page in the ranking leaderboard.

## Time Expectation

New pages usually need time. Do not expect a new page to rank immediately for mature keywords.

Operational expectation:

- New or low-competition keywords may move quickly.
- Mature keywords may need weeks or months.
- Competitive commercial keywords may need sustained internal links, content updates, and backlinks for three to four months.

## ProductViews-Specific Focus

ProductViews should stay focused on ecommerce product image generation.

Preferred primary keyword clusters:

- product angle generator
- generate product images from different angles
- ecommerce product angle images
- front side back product images
- product image generator without photoshoot
- AI product photography for Shopify
- Amazon product image angles
- Shopify product image checklist

Avoid diluting core pages with broad unrelated terms such as generic AI image, AI video, character art, or general creative tools unless the page is intentionally built for that separate intent.

## Release Checklist

Before shipping a new page or major page update, confirm:

- The page has one primary keyword cluster.
- The title, H1, meta description, and first section match that cluster.
- The page has a clear conversion action.
- The page links to the product/tool page where appropriate.
- Stronger internal pages link back to it.
- The sitemap includes it when it should be indexed.
- The page renders content correctly, not just layout chrome.
- The page does not mix unrelated big keywords.
- There is a backlink plan if the SERP is too competitive.

## Maintenance

SEO pages are not finished after publishing. Review important pages every month.

Update:

- Internal links
- Fresh examples
- Better CTAs
- New FAQs
- Comparison sections
- Product screenshots or demo images
- Backlink opportunities

Ranking is earned over time by focus, usefulness, authority, and patience.
